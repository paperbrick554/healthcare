About The Project

The Access Gap explores the severe shortage of primary care and family medicine physicians across underserved communities in America.

The Core Issues

Systemic barriers, lack of funding, and geographic isolation leave millions of patients without consistent medical options.

By The Numbers

Data from the CDC, HRSA, and AAMC highlights a widening disparity in patient-to-doctor ratios across rural and low-income urban zones.

Proposed Solutions

4.

Expanding Telehealth Capabilities

Virtual care visits dramatically improve access for rural patients and those with mobility limitations. With proper reimbursement structures, telehealth can genuinely complement in-person care.

5.

Team-based care models

Integrating nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and community health workers into family practices can extend reach without sacrificing quality — allowing one physician to serve a much larger patient population.

6.

Diversifying the physician workforce

Research shows physicians of color are more likely to practice in underserved communities. Targeted scholarships and pipeline programs can build a workforce that reflects and serves the full diversity of America.
